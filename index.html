What I do

Models, end to end.

Machine learning is the core of what I do. I've built models across the spectrum, from feedforward and convolutional networks through recurrent models and up to large transformers, and trained them on audio and video as much as on images and text. A lot of the real work lives upstream of the model: shaping multimodal data, building training pipelines, and scaling runs with mixed precision and distribution so experiments finish in hours, not days.

I care just as much about the half people skip, which is getting a trained model to actually serve. I deploy models behind NVIDIA Triton and optimize them with TensorRT, using operator fusion, quantization, and dynamic shapes to hit latency targets without giving up accuracy. At Nvidia I worked inside that inference stack directly, writing performance critical C++ and the compiler tooling that keeps results correct as the graph changes underneath.

Along the way I've trained generative models to synthesize data that's expensive or unsafe to collect, built computer vision that catches defects on a production line, and shipped recommendation systems driven by learned embeddings. In robotics I've fused inertial and visual signals for navigation when GPS isn't an option.

The foundation goes back to electrical engineering at UT Austin, signal processing and embedded systems, and computer science at Stanford, where I focused on generative models, vision, NLP, and compilers. I like understanding a system from the silicon up to the model on top, and reaching for math when a problem looks intractable.
