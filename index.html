About

A bit about me.

My instinct is to break a problem down to its fundamentals and rebuild it in software. That started with electrical engineering at UT Austin — signal processing, embedded systems, and how computers work at the level of circuits and architecture — and continued with computer science at Stanford, where I focused on systems, algorithms, and compilers.

I'm happiest working across the whole stack: writing clean, well-tested software, reasoning about performance and concurrency, and building the infrastructure and tooling that keeps systems running. I care about understanding a system end to end rather than treating any layer as a black box.

Outside of work I like puzzles, building small projects, and learning how the tools I use are put together. If you want to talk shop or collaborate, I'd love to hear from you.
